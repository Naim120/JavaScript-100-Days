Ballerina

A ballerina is a highly skilled dancer who specializes in the art of classical ballet. To become a ballerina, one must start training at a young age, usually around the age of six or seven. This early start is necessary to develop the flexibility, strength, and coordination required to execute the complex movements of ballet. Ballerinas possess a grace and poise that is unparalleled, and their movements are often described as fluid and effortless. Despite the demanding nature of the profession, ballerinas find great joy in their art, and their performances inspire and enchant audiences around the world.
