The Four Seasons

The four seasons are spring, summer, fall (also known as autumn), and winter. They are characterized by distinct changes in temperature, weather patterns, and daylight hours. Spring is a time of renewal and growth, while summer brings warmth and long days. Fall is known for its colorful foliage and harvest, and winter is marked by colder temperatures and shorter days.

Spring

Spring is the season of renewal and growth, marked by warmer temperatures, longer days, and budding trees and flowers. It's a time for new beginnings and fresh starts, as many animals emerge from hibernation and birds return from migration. Spring is also associated with Easter and other cultural and religious celebrations.

Summer

Summer is the season of warmth and relaxation, marked by longer days, sunny weather, and outdoor activities. It's a time for beach trips, barbecues, and family vacations, as well as festivals and concerts. Many people associate summer with ice cream, swimming pools, and lazy days spent in the sun.

Winter

Winter is the season of cold weather and snow, with shorter days and longer nights. It's a time for holiday celebrations, warm drinks, and cozy fireside gatherings. Outdoor activities include skiing, snowboarding, and ice skating. Winter also brings the magic of snow-covered landscapes and the excitement of the holiday season.

Fall

Fall, or autumn, is a season of colorful foliage and cooler weather. It's a time of harvest festivals, pumpkin patches, and apple picking. The shorter days and longer nights provide a perfect atmosphere for cozy nights in. Outdoor activities include hiking and biking in the crisp air. Fall is a time of change and transition, as nature prepares for the coming winter.
